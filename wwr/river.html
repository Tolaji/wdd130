White Water Rafting Information

White water rafting is an exhilarating water sport that involves navigating a river's turbulent sections in an inflatable raft. It's essential to choose a reputable outfitter, wear appropriate safety gear like life jackets and helmets, and follow the guide's instructions. Rapids are classified based on difficulty, ranging from Class I (easy) to Class V (extremely challenging). Popular rafting destinations include the Colorado River, Gauley River, and Zambezi River. Always check local regulations and conditions before planning a white water rafting adventure.

Special Trips

Rivers

Main Fork of the Salmon River, Idaho

Nestled deep in the Frank Church Wilderness of No Return winds the Salmon river. The Salmon was given the nickname long ago as the River of No Return by the people living near it because of the number of folks that came to visit and fell so in love with the rugged beauty of the river that they never left. Herds of elk and deer graze on the hillsides around the river and bear and bighorn sheep are also commonly sighted. The river corridor is also lined with awe inspiring ponderosa pine which tower over the sandy beaches in the campsites and provide shelter from sun or rain when needed. Let's not forget the rapids! They range from class I-IV. The Salmon is one of the longest undammed rivers in the United States. As a free flowing river the water is higher, faster, and colder in the early summer and lower, slower, and warmer in the late summer. There are good rapids all summer...but families with children who like to swim tend to appreciate the late summer's slower, warmer water more.

Desolation and Gray Canyon, Utah

Winding through canyons in central Utah, in some of the most desolate land anywhere, the Green river flows towards the mighty Colorado river and the sea. High grey stone cliffs, beautiful desert scenery, and great rapids have made this run famous. This 84-mile trip runs from from Sand Wash to Swaseys Rapid (just outside Green River Utah) and boasts outstanding scenery, interesting geologic formations, evidence of prehistoric and historic human activity, and great whitewater opportunities. Desolation is rated a Class II/III and is usually done in 6 days. Desolation Canyon has been recognized as a National Landmark. This designation is based on the uniqueness and rich history that the canyon tells.

Grand Canyon, Arizona

This is the grand daddy of all canyons and the grand daddy of all river trips. You will never forget your visit to the Grand Canyon. Grand Canyon river rafting combines world-class whitewater with breathtaking scenery to make one truly unforgettable river experience. The canyon is filled not only with exhilarating whitewater rapids, but with side canyons and ancient indian ruins accessible only by river. CLASS I - X: The Grand Canyon uses a unique river rating scale. The rapids in the Canyon are technically rated I - X ( 1 - 10) to accommodate such a wide variety of rapids and river variances. However, most people are familiar with the regular I - V river rating scale, so we often refer to both.

Courses

Flat Water Courses

White water rafting courses offer thrilling experiences for adventure seekers, navigating turbulent rapids amidst breathtaking natural landscapes. These courses provide a diverse range of challenges, catering to both beginners and seasoned enthusiasts. Flat water courses, specifically designed for beginners, offer a gentler introduction to the sport. Participants navigate calmer sections of rivers, mastering essential paddling techniques and teamwork. Instructors guide novices through the basics of river navigation and safety protocols, fostering confidence before progressing to more turbulent waters. These introductory courses not only impart foundational skills but also instill a profound appreciation for the beauty and power of rivers, making them an ideal starting point for those new to white water rafting.

White Water Courses

White water rafting courses offer thrilling experiences for adventure enthusiasts. Participants navigate tumultuous river rapids, mastering the art of teamwork and paddling techniques. These courses provide a dynamic learning environment, emphasizing safety while ensuring an adrenaline-fueled journey through challenging waters. Instructors guide participants through the intricacies of reading river currents, executing maneuvers, and fostering a deep appreciation for the power of nature. Whether it's tackling Class III rapids for beginners or conquering the formidable Class V rapids for the more experienced, white water courses promise an exhilarating blend of skill development and immersive outdoor excitement.

School Holiday

White water rafting courses during school holidays offer an exhilarating and educational adventure for thrill-seeking youngsters. These specialized programs combine the excitement of navigating turbulent rapids with essential water safety and teamwork skills. Participants not only experience the rush of conquering challenging river currents but also gain valuable knowledge in rafting techniques and river navigation. Instructors, certified in both rafting and education, create a dynamic learning environment where participants build confidence, communication skills, and a deep appreciation for nature. These school holiday courses provide a unique blend of adrenaline-pumping fun and educational engagement, making them an ideal choice for adventurous students looking to make lasting memories.

Stand Up Paddle Boarding

White water rafting courses provide exhilarating experiences as participants navigate through turbulent river waters, mastering teamwork and developing essential paddling skills. These courses cater to both beginners and seasoned adventurers, offering diverse levels of difficulty to suit varying comfort levels. Participants not only learn to navigate rapids but also gain insights into river safety, rescue techniques, and the dynamic art of reading water currents. For those seeking a unique aquatic adventure, stand-up paddleboarding (SUP) courses offer a serene yet invigorating alternative. Paddlers glide atop calm waters, honing balance and paddle techniques while enjoying breathtaking scenery, making SUP a versatile and enjoyable water sport for enthusiasts of all levels.

British Canoe Union

White water rafting courses offered by the British Canoe Union (BCU) provide enthusiasts with a thrilling and educational experience. As a recognized leader in water sports education, the BCU designs its courses to impart essential skills for navigating challenging river environments. Participants delve into the intricacies of white water rafting, mastering techniques for paddling through turbulent rapids, ensuring safety, and fostering teamwork. The BCU's commitment to quality instruction and adherence to safety standards make their courses an ideal choice for both beginners seeking an introduction to the exhilarating world of white water rafting and experienced paddlers looking to enhance their skills under expert guidance.

National Raft Guide School

The National Raft Guide School offers comprehensive and immersive white water rafting courses designed to equip participants with the skills and knowledge necessary for excelling in the dynamic world of river guiding. Focused on safety, technical proficiency, and leadership, the program covers a spectrum of topics ranging from mastering rapid navigation techniques to understanding river dynamics. Led by experienced instructors, the school provides hands-on training in real river environments, ensuring that students develop the confidence and competence needed to guide rafts through challenging white water scenarios. This course not only hones technical abilities but fosters a deep appreciation for responsible and enjoyable river navigation.

First Aid

White water rafting courses often prioritize safety, and First Aid training is a crucial component of ensuring participants' well-being on challenging river adventures. In these courses, participants learn essential life-saving skills, including assessing and responding to injuries, managing medical emergencies, and performing CPR. The dynamic nature of white water environments necessitates a quick and effective response to potential accidents or injuries. By incorporating First Aid training into white water rafting courses, participants gain the knowledge and confidence needed to handle unforeseen situations, fostering a safer and more enjoyable experience on the thrilling rapids.

Mountain Training Awards

White water rafting courses that include Mountain Training Awards provide participants with an exhilarating and skill-enhancing experience. These courses, often set against the backdrop of breathtaking mountainous landscapes, offer a unique blend of adventure and professional training. As part of the Mountain Training Awards, participants not only navigate the challenges of white water rapids but also acquire essential mountaineering and leadership skills. This comprehensive approach ensures that enthusiasts not only master the technical aspects of rafting but also develop a broader skill set for safely navigating and leading expeditions in mountainous terrains, making the courses an enriching and well-rounded adventure.
